Hidden in the City of London

The Passage That Inspired Diagon Alley

"Step through Ship Tavern Passage — the narrow Victorian alleyway beside Leadenhall Market that inspired the magical thoroughfare of J.K. Rowling's world."

Adjacent to Bull's Head Passage, where the entrance to Diagon Alley was filmed in Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, lies a compact, intimate barbershop unlike any other in the City. Just three chairs. No queues, no crowds — only the quiet precision of master craftsmen at work.

The Kingsmen Barbers is not merely a barbershop. It is a private grooming club for gentlemen who understand that appearance is armour, and every detail matters.

For the City

Corporate Grooming for City Professionals

"Seven o'clock in the morning, before the markets open. Your tie is knotted, your shirt is pressed — but your edge starts in the chair."

We open at 7 AM specifically for City professionals who understand that preparation is everything. Whether you're heading into a board meeting at Lloyd's, a client lunch at The Ned, or a pitch across the river, The Kingsmen ensures you arrive looking — and feeling — impeccable.

Our membership tiers are designed for the pace of the City. Same-day bookings from The Monarch tier up, dedicated barbers for Viceroy members, and no-wait priority access for The Regent.
